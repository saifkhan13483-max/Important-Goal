The most important design implications from your answers
A. Your hero needs sharper wording
Because “system” is vague, your homepage should define it immediately.

Instead of:

Try something closer to:

Stop starting over. Build a tiny daily system that still works on hard days.

or

For people who keep starting over: turn one goal into a daily system with a minimum action and a backup plan.

That is much clearer.

B. Minimum action should lead the product, not hide inside onboarding
Right now it’s Step 4.

I think it may need to become the main hook.

Possible framing:

“What’s the version you can still do on your worst day?”
“Your system only works if it survives bad days.”
“Set the minimum that keeps you in motion.”
That is much more emotionally resonant for your audience.

C. Fallback should be presented as intelligence, not failure insurance
Don’t frame it like:

“What if you fail?”
Frame it like:

“What does this system do when life gets messy?”
“How should your system adapt on low-energy days?”
“What keeps the habit alive when full effort isn’t possible?”
This preserves identity and reduces shame.

D. Recovery should branch by cause
You already see this.

That means recovery probably needs categories like:

low energy
forgot
time crunch
travel
sick
emotionally overwhelmed
Each should produce a different next suggestion.

That is where your product starts to feel truly smart.
